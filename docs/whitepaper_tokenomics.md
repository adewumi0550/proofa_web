## 4. The Scarcity Engine: Burn-on-License

The core value driver of $PRFA is its deflationary nature, tied directly to the success of its users.

### 4.1 The Mechanism
Whenever a creator successfully mints a **Human License** (achieving a high Human Score), a portion of the transaction fee is **permanently burned** (removed from circulation).

> **Formula:** `Total Supply` = `Initial Supply` - (`Licenses Issued` * `Burn Rate`)

### 4.2 Economic Impact
*   **Inverse Correlation:** As the *quantity* of verified human works increases, the *supply* of $PRFA decreases.
*   **Value Accrual:** This creates a scarcity shock. If Proofa becomes the standard for content verification, millions of licenses will be issued, significantly reducing supply while demand for the token (to pay for those licenses) rises.
*   **Sustainability:** Unlike "burn-on-transaction" models which can discourage use, "Burn-on-License" celebrates success. The token becomes scarcer only when the platform delivers value (a verified license).

## 6. Legal Framework & MiCA Compliance

To ensure long-term viability and operation within the European Union, $PRFA is structured to comply with the **Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA)** regulation.

### 6.1 Token Classification
Under MiCA Art. 3(9), $PRFA is classified as a **Utility Token**.
*   **Definition:** It is intended to provide digital access to a good or service (The Gemini Judge and Verification Ledger) available on DLT.
*   **Non-Financial:** It is explicitly *not* an E-Money Token (EMT) or Asset-Referenced Token (ART). It does not represent a claim on the issuer's debt or peg to a fiat currency.

### 6.2 Consumer Protection
*   **Whitepaper Standards:** This document conforms to MiCA Article 4 requirements, fully disclosing the project roadmap, risks, and rights.
*   **No Profit Expectation:** $PRFA is marketed based on its utility for verification services, not as a speculative investment vehicle.
*   **Issuer Transparency:** Proofa (the legal entity) maintains full transparency regarding the "Computational Floor" pricing logic, ensuring users understand the fair market value of the services.
